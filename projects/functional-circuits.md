# Abstract
Classical computing languages are (or at least used to be) split into two kinds, object-oriented and functional.
In functional programming languages, functions are no different to states, in that they too can be passed in as inputs to other functions.

In this work, we're seeking to explore the same concepts in quantum computations.
The questions we're dealing with include:
- How can quantum functions be treated as inputs and outputs to other (higher-order) quantum functions?
- Which higher-order quantum functions are forbidden?
  - How can we avoide these restrictions?
- How can we compose many quantum functions together?
  - How can this be done in a type-safe way?

These are just some of the questions that we are tackling, and are looking to publish our results this spring.
